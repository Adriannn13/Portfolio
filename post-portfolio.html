Building with Minimalism: The Portfolio Story

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."

What I Did

When I started building this portfolio, I made a clear decision to keep things simple and remove any unnecessary “noise.” I focused on clean typography and proper spacing to make everything easy to read and visually balanced. Using Tailwind CSS, I stuck to a simple 4-color palette—white, dark zinc for dark mode, blue for highlights, and a soft gray for borders.

I also used Lucide icons because of their thin, clean lines, which match well with the Inter font. Every detail—from the rounded corners to the subtle glass effect in the navigation—was designed to feel modern, polished, and lightweight.

Key Insight

Minimalism isn’t just about having less—it’s about making sure everything on the screen serves a purpose. A clean, well-organized design builds instant trust with potential employers or clients because it shows you can turn complex information into something simple and easy to understand.

Moving Forward

This project taught me that the hardest part of development isn’t adding more features—it’s knowing what to leave out. Moving forward, I want to carry this user-first approach into my work with PHP and Laravel, making sure the backend is just as clean, efficient, and well-organized as the frontend.
